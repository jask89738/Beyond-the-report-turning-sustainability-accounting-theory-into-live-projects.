Three project blueprints
Each prompt is structured for a research team, a corporate sustainability department, or a consultancy. Pick the lens that matches your mandate.

Option 1 · Consulting / Internal
Beyond the Report: Diagnosing the Organizational "Missing Link"
Develop a proprietary diagnostic tool that assesses an organization's 'Internal Management Readiness' — mapping the organizational dynamics that translate disclosure into operational change. Output: a heat map from 'Compliance Reporter' to 'Transformative Integrator.'

1
Identify the 4–5 key organizational dynamics (executive buy-in, middle-management autonomy, financial integration, incentive structures).
2
Design a survey and interview protocol to locate the organization on each dynamic.
3
Pilot the audit with one high-, one mid-, and one low-performer to validate.
4
Build an intervention matrix mapping each diagnosis to concrete fixes.
Option 2 · Data Analytics / Framing
Deconstructing the "E" in ESG: A Contextual Analysis of Accounting Choices
Using the lens of 'contextualizing and financializing objects', analyze how a single non-monetary issue (Water Scarcity, Scope 3 commuting, biodiversity) is represented across 50 leading companies. Build a taxonomy of accounting narratives that exposes where accounting is transformative versus merely performative.

1
Select a 'fuzzy' object that resists standardization (biodiversity, social value).
2
Scrape sustainability reports from 50 companies as your corpus.
3
Code definitions, measurements, and exclusions along an Accounting Spectrum: Narrative → Qualitative → Quantitative → Financial Proxy.
4
Publish a Representation Report showing how framing changes perceived performance.
Option 3 · Strategy / Training
The Standardization Pipeline: A Playbook for Embedding Emerging Accounting Phenomena
CSRD and ISSB are forcing rapid standardization, with the risk that companies treat them as compliance checklists rather than cognitive triggers. Build a Transformative Playbook that walks a new metric from Abstract Concern → Data Collection → Financial Materiality → Operational Procedure.

1
Map the stakeholders a new standard touches: procurement, R&D, IR, legal.
2
Translate a CSRD metric into a decision-making KPI for each group.
3
Design workshop simulations forcing teams to solve a business problem with the new data.
4
Pilot the playbook with a client and measure shifts in strategic decisions.

The 30-Day "Signal vs. Noise" Audit
A live, four-week protocol to test the Missing Link hypothesis against real reports and real capital decisions.

Week 1
Select & Map
Pick 10 public sustainability reports. List each company's stated material topics and KPIs.

Week 2
Decision Audit
Ask a contact (or use capex disclosures) which metrics actually drove capital allocation or procedure change last year.

Week 3
Signal vs. Noise
Analyze the delta between what is measured and what is managed. Surface barriers: data latency, no internal pricing, silos.

Week 4
Diagnostic Dashboard
Build a dashboard that visualizes the gap. Present back with the thesis: if it isn't managed, it isn't really measured.

Why these prompts
Respect the Druckerian myth
Measurement is treated as necessary but insufficient — focus shifts to the organizational dynamics that turn data into change.

Leverage the accounting vantage point
Instead of measuring performance, study how accounting reshapes reality — contextualizing and financializing the objects it touches.

Bridge academia and practice
Each prompt forces a theoretical framework to solve a practical problem: making reporting useful inside the firm.
